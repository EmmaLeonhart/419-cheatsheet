The Problem of Sequential Data
в–ӘStandard Feed-Forward NNs assume inputs and outputs are 
fixed-size tensors
в–ӘIn reality, data can be sequential and variable in length:
в–ӘNLP: Sentences of varying word counts
в–ӘTime-Series: Stock prices over arbitrary timeframes
в–ӘCore failure of FFNNs: Lack of memory 
в–ӘProcessing рқ‘Ҙрқ‘Ўhappens independently of рқ‘Ҙрқ‘ЎвҲ’1
в–ӘThis means the model is memoryless, i.e. it has no 
memory of anything before.
вҖў sometimes long-distance context can be important such as 
in the context of NLP (you need the past words to 
understand the current one)

Evaluating LMs: Perplexity
в–ӘIn NLP, we evaluate Language Models using Perplexity.
в–ӘPerplexity is the inverse probability of the corpus, normalized by 
the number of words.
в–Әcorpus is a large, structured dataset of text or spoken 
language (e.g., the complete works of Shakespeare)
в–ӘIt is equivalent to the exponentiation of the Cross-Entropy loss:
Perplexity = exp рқҗҪрқңғ
where рқҗҪрқңғ: The loss function (typically cross-entropy) parameterized by рқңғ
в–ӘLower perplexity indicates the model is less 'confused' by test 
data.

Traditional Approach: n-gram LMs
в–ӘAn n-gram is a consecutive chunk of n tokens.
в–ӘTo simplify, we make a Markov assumption:
в–Әрқ‘Ҙрқ‘ӣdepends ONLY on the preceding (рқ‘ӣвҲ’1) words.

The Asymmetry Problem
в–ӘThe "Fixed-Window" approach is inefficient: it treats the exact 
same word differently just because it appears in a different spot 
in the sentence. This is known as The Asymmetry Problem.
в–ӘIn this model, the large weight matrix рқ‘Ҡis essentially chopped 
into sections (sub-matrices). 
в–ӘPosition 1 (рқ‘’1) is multiplied by рқ‘Ҡ1 .Position 2 (рқ‘’2) is multiplied 
by рқ‘Ҡ2 . Even if the word "apple" appears in both positions, the 
math used to process it is completely different because рқ‘Ҡ1
and рқ‘Ҡ2 are separate sets of numbers. 
в–ӘThere is NO parameter sharing across time steps.
в–ӘThe window is rigidly fixed.
в–ӘIf the subject of a sentence was mentioned 10 words ago, and 
window size is 5, the model has literally zero mathematical 
access to the subject.
в–ӘExpanding the window size substantially increases parameter 
counts.

Recurrent Neural Network (RNN)
в–ӘThe RNN is an architecture designed specifically to fulfill all 
sequence requirements.
в–ӘCore concept: Introduce a Hidden State в„Һрқ‘Ўthat evolves over 
time.
в–ӘThe network applies a continuous recurrence formula at every 
discrete time step.
в–ӘCrucially, the exact same weights are reused at every single 
step рқ’•.

в–ӘTherefore, в„Һрқ‘Ўtheoretically contains information from xрқ‘Ў, xрқ‘ЎвҲ’1, вҖҰ , back to x1
в–Әв„Һрқ‘Ўacts as the network internal short-term memory

в–ӘBecause parameters are shared across time, a loss at time t depends on the 
hidden state at time t, which depends on t-1, and so on.
в–ӘWe must backpropagate the error signal backwards through the unrolled 
temporal graph.
в–ӘThis specific application of backpropagation is called Backpropagation 
Through Time (BPTT).

BPTT: The Multivariate Chain Rule
в–ӘIf a variable depends on multiple intermediate paths, its 
derivative is the sum of the derivatives along all paths.
в–ӘIn an RNN, the matrix рқ‘Ҡв„Һв„Һ is used at step 1, step 2, ..., step t.
в–ӘTo find the effect of рқ‘Ҡв„Һв„Һ on the loss at time рқ‘Ў, we must sum its

в–ӘThe ResNet Highway
в–ӘResNets solve vanishing gradients in deep

в–ӘThe additive +рқ‘Ҙ creates an identity highway where

в–ӘLSTMs use a remarkably similar additive
